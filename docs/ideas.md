# Voxel Engine — Ideas Brainstorm (Round 1)

Generated by a 5-agent swarm: gameplay designer, tech architect, rendering engineer,
physics engineer, and Mario specialist. Engine-first focus; Mario is a side feature.

---

## Gameplay (Engine-First)

### 1. SIEGE & BREACH — Asymmetric Fortification Warfare
Build phase with material budget (stone=strong, sand=weak). Attack phase with the 4
destruction tools. Sever a load-bearing pillar and the whole wall detaches as a
rigidbody and tumbles. Connectivity flood is the hero — no HP bar. Hot-seat prototype
works today, no engine changes. Slots 5-9 reserved for relic-grab tool.

### 2. VOXEL RALLY — Destructible Procedural Track Racing
Terrain gen creates the track, destruction tools let you carve shortcuts, but every
shortcut leaves a crater for the next lap. Fallen trees become real debris obstacles
the solver rolls around. Time-trial mode buildable now; full racing needs vehicle
controller + streaming.

### 3. STORM SURVIVOR — Weather vs. Your Shelter
Day: gather, build shelter choosing materials by strength. Night: storm rolls in,
wind applies lateral impulse to exposed blocks, flood water rises and carves channels
through weak sand, lightning ignites wood roofs. Connectivity-to-ground determines
if shelter holds. Wind-only prototype works today; full weather needs vox-sim.

### 4. Creative Building with Structural Integrity
Free-build mode where material strength matters. Build towers, bridges, castles —
but they obey real physics. Overload a beam and it cracks. Build too tall without
support and it collapses. The destruction pipeline becomes the building validator.

### 5. Destruction Derby
Multiplayer (or AI) arena where players blast structures at each other. Bombs,
lasers, debris — all the tools, but pointed at each other's fortifications. Last
structure standing wins. Needs networking or AI opponents.

## Technical Systems

### 6. vox-sim — Cellular Automata (fire / water / falling sand) — 🔄 PARTIAL (fire done)
New sibling crate at physics tier. Depends on vox-core + vox-world. Per-voxel state
as sidecar arrays (not packed into Voxel — design doc §4 reserves this). Fire
spreads through flammable materials, water flows downhill and carves channels,
falling sand cascades. Scale-invariant (rates in m/s). Headlessly testable.
**Highest single-system impact on the roadmap.**

### 7. vox-stream — Streaming Infinite Worlds
New crate at world/gen tier. HashMap chunk storage is already streaming-ready.
Deterministic per-chunk generation. Background GenQueue mirroring RemeshQueue.
Replaces finite world wall with load radius. Turns the demo into a sandbox.

### 8. vox-save — Chunk Serialization & Save/Load
New crate at world tier. Uniform chunks = ~3 bytes, Dense = palette-RLE.
Delta-over-base: only edited chunks saved, unedited regenerate from seed.
Versioned header for future features. The Uniform/Dense enum was designed for this.

### 9. vox-eco — Ecosystem & Creatures
New crate at gen/physics tier. Creatures with simple AI (wander, flee, hunt).
Populations that grow and die. Could spawn enemies for Storm Survivor, opponents
for Voxel Rally, or wildlife. Typed arenas grow into ECS when needed.

### 10. Weather & Day/Night System — ✅ DONE
Rotating sun via Tunables + egui. Modulates sky color, ambient, sun strength, fog.
Wind applies impulses to debris bodies. Rain could feed the water sim. Foundation
that shadows and volumetric fog depend on.

## Rendering

### 11. Grass Sway & Animated Foliage — ✅ DONE (3D blade geometry + wind sway)
Displace grass-top faces in vertex shader using world XZ + time. No vertex format
change, no new pass. Uses spare CameraUniform.fog.w as time uniform. Cheap,
immediate visual win.

### 12. Day/Night Sky + Sun Uniform — ✅ DONE (subsumed by #10, all uniforms threaded)
Replace hardcoded sun_dir with time-of-day-driven rotation. Modulate SKY_COLOR,
AMBIENT_SKY, SUN_STRENGTH, CLEAR_COLOR by sun elevation. Prerequisite for shadow
mapping and volumetric fog.

### 13. Transparent Water Voxels with Refraction — ✅ DONE (alpha 0.85, blue tint, depth_write off)
Requires material schema change (new render flags in MaterialDef + TOML + storage
buffer). Second blended pass sorted back-to-front. Fake refraction via sin ripple
on fog mix avoids framebuffer sampling. First structural new render pass.

### 14. Cascaded Directional Shadow Mapping — ✅ DONE (2048x2048, PCF 3x3, ortho sun cam)
Depth-only pass from sun's viewpoint, reusing draw_chunks/draw_bodies with depth
bias. Main pass samples with PCF 3x3. Cascades via array texture. Most invasive
rendering change, depends on day/night sun uniform.

### 15. HDR Post-Processing (tone mapping + bloom + SSAO + volumetric fog) — ✅ DONE (offscreen Rgba16Float + cel-shading + saturation/grading; bloom/SSAO/volumetric fog are future phases)
Rgba16Float offscreen intermediate as new voxel-pass target, then fullscreen post
pass. Current direct-to-sRGB path makes tone mapping impossible without this.
SSAO needs depth texture binding + normals MRT. Structural keystone that unlocks
everything.

## Physics & Simulation

### 16. Chain-Reaction Explosives & Blast-Cascade System
Add `explosive` flag to MaterialDef. Blast scans carved voxels for explosive
materials, enqueues deferred secondary blasts. Genuine cascades through the
existing destruction pipeline. Bomb Block as buildable material (hotbar slot 5).
Cheap, high payoff — reuses everything.

### 17. Sustained-Load Structural Failure (Towers Topple Under Load)
New stress analysis alongside the connectivity flood. Towers under their own weight
or heavy debris piles yield and collapse. NOT in the existing flood (binary
anchored/bounded) or impact pipeline (filters resting forces). Genuinely new
physics: beams crack, towers topple.

### 18. Fluid Simulation (SPH or grid-based water)
Water as particles or grid cells with pressure/velocity. Flows downhill, fills
containers, erodes terrain. Pairs with vox-sim for cellular automata approach, or
standalone for particle-based fluids.

### 19. Terrain Erosion from Water Flow
Water carves channels through weak materials over time. Sand washes away, stone
resists. Creates natural canyons and riverbeds. The carve pipeline already exists —
erosion is a slow, automated version driven by fluid sim.

### 20. Buoyancy & Floating Structures — ✅ DONE (from upstream merge, debris floats)
Debris bodies float or sink based on material density vs water density. Wood floats
(density 700), stone sinks (density 2600). Build a raft, watch it bob. Extends the
existing rigidbody solver with buoyant force calculation.

## Mario Side Features

### 21. Wall Collision via Surface Objects — ✅ DONE (all 6 FACES, surface objects)
Use libsm64's sm64_surface_object_create/move/delete API for wall collision instead
of the monolithic static surface array. Incremental cell-based updates. Unlocks
wall-kicks, wall-slides, ledge grabs — the biggest gameplay gap for Mario.

### 22. SM64 Audio Integration — ✅ DONE (cpal resampled 32→48kHz, volume 0.5)
Wire sm64_audio_init + sm64_audio_tick for Mario's sound effects (jump "wahoo!",
footsteps, ground pound) and background music. FFI already declared but unused.
Would add huge personality to the mode.

### 23. Mario ↔ Debris Interaction — ✅ DONE (surface objects for debris)
Register awake debris bodies as SM64 surface objects so Mario can stomp falling
chunks (they shatter, he bounces), kick them (impulse), or ride them as they fall.
Bridges the two physics systems.

### 24. Cap Throw from Odyssey
Cappy as a projectile that interacts with voxels — could carve a small hole, knock
debris around, or be thrown to possess/mount debris bodies. New tool slot, new
animation state.

### 25. Power-Up Voxel Materials
Metal cap (immune to fire, sinks in water), wing cap (fly around), vanish cap (clip
through walls). Implemented as special voxel materials Mario interacts with — touch
a metal block, get metal cap. Ties Mario to the voxel material system.

## Mario Side Features (Round 2)

Grounding audit before the pitches. The current integration:
`vox_physics::blast(world, phys, registry, center_m, radius_m, power,`
`seed)` already chains carve → `detach_unsupported` → `apply_blast_impulse`
→ debris chips (destruction.rs:662) — the full bomb pipeline in one call.
The Rust `MarioTickResult` (lib.rs:279-299) surfaces `position, velocity,
face_angle, forward_velocity, health, action, anim_id, anim_frame, flags`
but **drops** the C struct's `invincTimer` and `particleFlags`
(ffi.rs:68-69) — those need wrapper additions, not just reads. Bound FFI
setters are only `sm64_set_mario_action/_position/_velocity/_faceangle`
(ffi.rs:139-142); `sm64_set_mario_invincibility`-style setters are NOT
bound. Action consts like `ACT_GROUND_POUND` (0x008008A9),
`ACT_GROUND_POUND_LAND` (0x0080023C), `ACT_TRIPLE_JUMP` (0x01000882)
exist in `libsm64/decomp/include/sm64.h` but have NO Rust `const`s — they
must be added to ffi.rs. libsm64 does NOT expose object/star interaction
to the host, and only accepts `camLookX/camLookZ` (mario.rs:233-238); the
internal Lakitu cam is unreachable. Round 1 covered wall collision, audio,
debris interaction, cap throw, power-up materials — these five don't
overlap.

### 26. Star Power-Up — Invincible Terrain Plow
SM64's star (invincible + fast) becomes a *destructive* power-up: while
invincible, Mario plows through terrain instead of stopping at it. Each
tick call `vox_physics::blast` at Mario's feet with a small radius
(~0.5 m, scaled by `MarioTickResult::forward_velocity` so a sprinting
star-Mario carves a wider trench than a walking one). `blast` already
runs the full carve→detach→impulse→chips pipeline, so severing a
load-bearing pillar while star-running topples everything it held up —
genuine chain destruction from just running through a wall. The carve
trench is the real visual; the SM64 sparkle trail is NOT wired today (see
gaps below).

**Real FFI/wrapper gaps — this idea needs two new FFI bindings, not
just wiring:** (1) To *grant* a star from a voxel pickup, call
`sm64_set_mario_invincibility(marioId, timer)` (libsm64.h:172) for the
invincibility and `sm64_set_mario_forward_velocity(marioId, vel)`
(libsm64.h:171) for the speed boost — both are in libsm64.h but NOT bound
in ffi.rs:139-142 (only action/position/velocity/faceangle are). NOTE:
there is no `MARIO_STAR_POWER` flag — the MARIO_* set (sm64.h:115-136) is
caps only (NORMAL/VANISH/METAL/WING…), and star invincibility is the
`invincTimer` i16 field driving `sInvulnerable` (fake_interaction.c:39,55),
not a flags bit. `sm64_set_mario_state` (libsm64.h:166) exists but sets cap
flags, so it's NOT the star grant. Bind the invincibility + forward-
velocity pair; this is the one genuinely new FFI surface of the five.
(2) To *detect* "is Mario currently invincible" each tick: since YOU call
`sm64_set_mario_invincibility` to grant it, track the remaining timer
host-side (decrement per tick in `MarioMode`) — no read-back of the C
`SM64MarioState.invincTimer` (which is dropped by `MarioTickResult`,
lib.rs:279-299) and no wrapper extension needed at all. (3) The sparkle
trail is misleading to promise: `render()` (mario.rs:287-299)
only draws `MarioGeometry` (positions/normals/colors/uvs) and
`mario_pipeline` has no particle pass — so the sparkle needs new render
work or is simply dropped; the carve trench is the visible effect either
way. (4) Add the star/action consts to ffi.rs. Pickup: a "Star Block"
voxel material or a rare drop from shattered debris (`carve_body_*`
already returns removed voxels — roll a chance there). Self-limits by the
invincibility timer and the debris budget. Heaviest of the five, but the
only one that makes an SM64 *power-up* feel like a voxel-engine *verb*.

### 27. Mario as a Fifth Destruction Tool — Mario Bomb — ✅ DONE (ACT_GROUND_POUND_LAND → blast)
Strongest, fully feasible today. Mario is already toggleable with M; this
makes him *useful* in the non-Mario sandbox too. Detect
`ACT_GROUND_POUND_LAND` (0x0080023C, sm64.h:208) from
`MarioTickResult::action` (already surfaced, no wrapper change), then
call the existing `vox_physics::blast(world, phys, registry, mario_feet,
radius, power, seed)` — that one function already spawns the crater,
detaches unsupported material, applies the blast impulse, and scatters
debris chips. A ground pound *is* a bomb you aim with a jump.

**Only new code:** add `ACT_GROUND_POUND`/`_LAND` consts to ffi.rs, and
an `if state.action == ACT_GROUND_POUND_LAND` branch in the Mario tick
that calls `blast` once per pound (guard against re-firing on the same
pound with a "last fired action" latch). Radius/power can reuse
`Tunables::blast_power` and the bomb tool's radius. Optional upgrade:
hold-Z-before-landing (detected via `button_z` held across the
`ACT_GROUND_POUND` → `_LAND` transition) widens the radius. Optional
meta-feature: a hotbar slot-5 "spawn marker" (slot 5 is already
reserved, tools.rs:11) lets the player place Mario as a one-shot
demolition charge they *aim with gameplay* rather than a crosshair.
Reuses 100% of the bomb pipeline; reframes Mario as a character-shaped
tool.

### 28. Lakitu Camera Modes — Fly Cam & Fixed-Camera Angles
SM64's signature feel comes from Lakitu's inertia and mode switches, but
libsm64 only accepts `camLookX/camLookZ` (mario.rs:233-238) — the
internal Lakitu cam is NOT exposed to the host. So this is a *voxel-side
camera controller*, not a tap into SM64 internals. Add a `MarioCamMode`
enum cycled with C: (1) **Lakitu fly cam** — add spring-damped lag to the
existing `cam_yaw`/`cam_pitch`/position math in `MarioMode`: the camera
position eases toward its target offset each frame, so quick turns swing
it with weight instead of snapping; pull it out of walls with the already-
bound `sm64_surface_find_wall_collision` (ffi.rs:130). (2) **Fixed cam** —
a stationary anchor: a list of `(AABB region, pos, look_at)` presets; when
Mario enters a region the camera locks to that anchor, giving cinematic
platformer framing over destructed structures. (3) **Free orbit**
(current behavior, kept as default). All three reuse `MarioCameraUniform`;
pure Rust camera logic, no FFI, no new render pass. The fixed-cam presets
author as a `Vec` in Tunables, editable in the egui panel (vox-debug).

### 29. Collectibles Placed in the Voxel World — Coins & Stars
libsm64 does NOT expose SM64's native object/coin/star system to the
host — so this is **entirely voxel-engine-side**, not tapping SM64
internals. Maintain a `Vec<Collectible { pos: Vec3, kind, collected }>`
in `MarioMode`. Each tick, proximity-check `MarioTickResult::position`
(already surfaced) against each uncollected item; within radius → mark
collected, increment a counter. Coins render as small instanced quads (or
simple billboards) in the Mario render pass; a counter goes in the egui
overlay (vox-debug already has `selected_material`/`tool_radius` slots to
mirror). **Stars** are the headline: place a Star at a hard-to-reach spot
(top of a tower the player must build then climb, or across a gap
requiring destruction to expose a path). Collecting a star can trigger
#26's star power-up for a few seconds (pending #26's binding work) *and*
shows a "course clear" flourish. Placement is authoring, not systems:
a TOML/JSON list of `{pos, kind}` spawned on Mario-mode enter. Optional:
`carve_body_*` returns removed voxels — roll a small chance to drop a
coin where debris shatters, rewarding the wrecking-ball playstyle. This
gives Mario-mode a *goal* (collect N stars) for the first time, all with
position checks and a counter. No new FFI.

### 30. Ground-Pound Craters — Mario as a Terrain Sculptor — ✅ DONE (carve_sphere + triple-jump mound)
Distinct from #27's *explosive* pound (which uses `blast` and spawns
debris): this is the *constructive* version where the pound edits terrain
*gently* for traversal and sculpting. A ground pound carves a shallow
hemispherical dent via `vox_physics::carve_sphere` (destruction.rs:92)
with a small radius (~0.8 m) — critically *not* `blast`, so no
`apply_blast_impulse`, no `detach_unsupported`, no chain destruction, just
a shaped dent. Repeated pounds dig deeper stair-shaped holes, letting
Mario *dig his own stairs* into cliffs — SM64-style platforming carved
into voxel terrain. Conversely, a triple-jump-then-pound (detect
`ACT_TRIPLE_JUMP` 0x01000882, sm64.h:258, in the action history) *raises*
a voxel mound at the impact point (a single `World::set` place), turning
Mario into a low-rent builder for making ledges to reach collectibles
(#29).

**New code:** add `ACT_GROUND_POUND`/`_LAND`/`ACT_TRIPLE_JUMP` consts to
ffi.rs (they live in sm64.h but have no Rust bindings), and two action-
gated branches in the Mario tick — `carve_sphere` for the pound, a
`World::set` place for the triple-jump-pound. `carve_sphere` and voxel
placement both already exist; no new physics. Two terrain-shaping verbs
(carve-down for descent/stairs, raise-up for ascent/ledges) pair with
#29 collectibles for platforming level design *inside* the destructible
world. The "Mario-shaped path" carved into a cliff face becomes a visible
record of how he climbed it.

## Gameplay (Round 2)

### 31. Controlled Demolition Engineering
Inverse of siege warfare — bring a tower down in a chosen direction with zero
collateral. Pure carve+connectivity+strength. Death Laser is the high-stakes
clean-slice tool. Scoring = debris footprint vs target zone after bodies sleep.
Works today.

### 32. Voxel Archaeology — Excavate Without Destroying
Destruction is the thing you AVOID. Fragile fossil artifacts shatter if blast/impact
exceeds their strength threshold. Dig=safe, Bomb/Laser=risk, Scalable Dig=tension.
Needs new material ("ceramic" strength 0.3) + ruin gen module.

### 33. Rube-Goldberg Demolition Machine
Player authors a multi-stage physical chain reaction: sever, detach, fall, impact,
fracture, sever again. No new explosive flag needed — composed loop of existing
systems. Material strength tunes each domino's topple threshold. Works today.

### 34. Time-Rewind Demolition Sandbox
Braid-for-destruction. Scrub timeline, fork from any prior state, try a different
cut. Every carve returns removed: Vec<(IVec3,Voxel)> — complete invertible log.
Undo = write pairs back + despawn seeded bodies. Needs vox-rewind crate for full
scrub; single-step undo prototype fits vox-app.

### 35. Voxel Seismograph — Mining With Cave-In Risk
The connectivity flood IS the gameplay loop — exposed as a cave-in warning readout.
Sever the last anchor and the roof falls on you. New flood_risk_probe reports
at-risk volumes without spawning bodies. Material strength = margin. Needs
read-only flood probe in vox-physics + vox-debug overlay.

## Technical Systems (Round 2)

### 36. vox-cmd — Command Queue and Tiered Undo/Redo — ✅ DONE (Tier 1: voxel diffs, Ctrl+Z/Y)
Two-tier undo. Tier 1: world-edit undo (voxel diffs, ~400 LOC). Tier 2: destruction
undo via physics snapshots (~600 LOC). edit_box already computes the diff but
discards it — the undo data is right there.

### 37. vox-edit — In-Engine Editor with Brush Tools and Prefab Stamping — ✅ DONE (E to toggle, LMB/RMB sphere brush)
Brush = operation (Paint/Replace/Erase/Smooth/Noise) x shape (Sphere/Box/Line/
Cylinder). Prefab stamping reuses VoxelSlab::extract. Editor is a separate mode
(toggled like Mario mode). Depends on #36 for undo.

### 38. vox-mod — Data-Driven Content and Tool API
Three tiers: (1) open-ended material properties HashMap (unblocks water, explosives,
flammable — currently blocked by deny_unknown_fields), (2) data-described tools as
TOML recipes composing native Rust effectors, (3) data-driven gen hooks. NO
scripting runtime — mods compose our primitives.

### 39. vox-determinism — Replay and Lockstep Networking Foundation — ✅ DONE (snapshot replay, R/P)
One system, not two. Replay (snapshot-based, ships first) and lockstep networking
(deterministic, ships second) share a single determinism seam. Prerequisite for
Destruction Derby (#5).

### 40. vox-gpu — GPU Compute Meshing
Move greedy meshing to GPU compute. Dual-path: keep vox-mesh as testable reference
backend, vox-gpu as opt-in GPU backend. Requires pipeline-mesh-store separation
first. Hot-reload shaders comes near-free as groundwork.

## Rendering (Round 2)

### 41. Screen-Space Reflections (SSR)
Reflection, not refraction. Needs the HDR color+depth sampling that #15 unlocks.
New pass, composes with water (#13).

### 42. GPU Particle System — 🔄 PARTIAL (ParticleSystem for smoke/debris; no separate crate)
New render-tier crate. Coarse voxel debris already exists but no fire/smoke/spark
layer. Emitters wire to existing destruction outcomes + ImpactEvents.

### 43. Progressive Block-Break Crack Decals — ✅ DONE (procedural shader, crack_intensity in ambient_sky.w)
Makes the PROCESS of destruction visible (pre-fracture damage), not just the result.
New pass + meshing extension. Pairs with sustained-load #17.

### 44. Voxel Cone-Traced Global Illumination
World-space color bleeding + directional indirect light. The only proposal that
makes light bounce. Most expensive; quality-gated. Needs storage textures feature.

### 45. Live Minimap / 3D Map Overlay
Navigation, not fidelity. The only HUD/spatial-awareness idea. Becomes essential
once streaming (#7) makes the world infinite. Small new pass + egui composite.

## Physics and Simulation (Round 2)

### 46. vox-rope — Rope, Chain and Cable Constraint Networks
New sibling crate at physics tier. Distance constraints between rigidbodies as a
first-class solver pass alongside contacts. Chains hang, swing, go taut, snap when
tension exceeds material strength. Gameplay: swing across chasms, wrecking balls,
chain-link bridges, grappling hook. Mario: grab chains and swing.

### 47. vox-cloth — Voxel Cloth Simulation
Mass-spring grid for flags, capes, tarps. Interacts with voxel world via existing
surface-point-vs-grid contacts. Wind force per node. Tearing when spring strain
exceeds material strength. New lightweight render path (not voxel mesher).

### 48. Temperature Diffusion (Heat Spreads Through Materials)
Per-voxel temperature sidecar (same pattern as vox-sim). Heat diffuses through
adjacent voxels based on material conductivity. Hot enough = ignite flammable
materials = fire sim. Cold enough = freeze water. Pairs with vox-sim #6.

### 49. Gravity Wells and Anti-Gravity Zones
Region-based gravity modification. Zero-G zones for floating debris. Gravity wells
that pull bodies toward a point. Inverted gravity for ceiling-walking. Simple:
override the gravity vector per-region in the solver. Big gameplay implications.

### 50. Earthquake / Tremor Events
Periodic impulses applied to ALL awake bodies + terrain carve at fault lines.
Creates cascade events where everything shakes, weak structures collapse, debris
rains down. Uses existing apply_impulse + carve pipeline at scale.

## Performance Critique (Grounded in Source Code) — ✅ DONE

### 51. CRITICAL: world_contacts does 7 HashMap lookups per surface point per substep
File: vox-physics/src/contact.rs, function world_contacts (lines 115-161).
For every awake body, every substep, each surface point calls world.solid() up to 7
times (point + 6 face neighbors), each doing a HashMap<IVec3, Chunk> lookup. The
SolidLookup cache (world.rs:21-51) already exists and is used by the flood fill but
NOT by world_contacts. Fix: thread and mut SolidLookup through world_contacts — 6 of
7 lookups hit the cached chunk, collapsing to ~1 hash lookup.

### 52. HIGH: candidate_pairs allocates HashMap + HashSet + Vec from scratch every substep
File: vox-physics/src/broadphase.rs, function candidate_pairs (lines 14-54).
Already uses a uniform grid (not O(n^2)), but allocates fresh HashMap, HashSet, and
Vec every call. Called 3x per step (2 substeps + islands). Fix: (a) hoist to
once-per-step (AABBs only integrate after substep), (b) reuse scratch buffers with
.clear() retaining capacity, (c) fold islands() call in with already-built pairs.

### 53. HIGH: substep allocates contacts Vec + rebuilds warm-start HashMap every substep
File: vox-physics/src/solver.rs, function substep (lines 323-477).
Fresh Vec<Contact> allocation every substep (Contact is ~100+ bytes). warm.clear()
+ repopulate HashMap every substep. Fix: reuse contacts Vec with .clear(), use
capacity-preserving clear for warm map.

### 54. MEDIUM: Mario surface generation scans all 32^3 voxels per chunk even when sparse
File: vox-sm64/src/surface.rs, greedy_faces_for_chunk (lines 141-179).
Dense chunks with few solid voxels still scan all 32k voxels. Fix: early-exit
per-slice when solid_count is small, or skip all-air/all-solid slices.

### 55. MEDIUM: draw_bodies iterates all body meshes without distance culling
File: vox-render/src/voxel_pipeline.rs, draw_bodies (lines 408-428).
Frustum culls body meshes, but no distance-based LOD or culling — a debris body
200m away still gets drawn. Fix: add max_draw_distance check alongside frustum.

### 56. LOW: edit_box doesn't batch dirty notifications
File: vox-world/src/world.rs, edit_box (lines 161-223).
Dirty chunk/region notifications are per-chunk, not batched. Large blasts create
redundant wake_region calls. Fix: batch dirty regions into one wake_region call.

### 57. LOW: Mario vertex buffer rewritten every frame even when idle
File: vox-render/src/mario_pipeline.rs, draw (lines 300-326).
queue.write_buffer every frame, even when Mario is idle and geometry hasn't changed.
Fix: version-stamp the geometry, skip upload when unchanged.

## Weapons & First-Person Combat (Round 3)

### 58. First-Person Weapon Viewmodel — Static Mesh
Add a first-person weapon viewmodel rendered in a separate pass with a tighter
FOV (~55° vs world's 70°). New WeaponPipeline in vox-render mirroring MarioPipeline
(camera uniform + texture + vertex/index buffers). New weapon.wgsl shader reusing
cel-shading lighting from voxel.wgsl. WeaponVertex: [pos, normal, uv] (28 bytes).
Render after water in the scene pass so it receives post-processing. Add gltf crate
dep for GLB loading. ~500 LOC, ~2 days. Foundation for all weapon features.

### 59. Skeletal Animation System for Weapon Viewmodels
Build a skeletal animation pipeline: SkinnedVertex (adds joint_indices + joint_weights,
56 bytes), bone hierarchy from GLTF skins, per-frame keyframe interpolation
(Quat::slerp for rotation, Vec3::lerp for translation), GPU vertex-shader skinning
(upload array<mat4x4<f32>, 256> uniform, 4-bone blend per vertex). Adapt Bevy's
skinning.wgsl skin_model() function. ~2000-3000 LOC, ~5-7 days on top of #58.
Unlocks animated reload/fire/aim/sprint.

### 60. Weapon Animation State Machine
Lightweight state machine: Idle → AimDownSight → Firing → Reloading → Inspect.
Cross-fade blending between states (slerp/lerp per-joint by blend factor). Wire to
input (RMB = ADS, LMB = fire, R = reload). Procedural recoil kick (pitch impulse),
weapon sway (lagged mouse_delta), view bob (sinusoidal from movement velocity).
~500-800 LOC on top of #59. glam provides all the math (Quat::slerp, Vec3::lerp).

### 61. Weapon Asset Pipeline — Bring Your Own (COD local / CC0 public)
Two-tier asset strategy mirroring the SM64 ROM approach:
**Local/personal**: Download pre-extracted COD weapon packs from Open3DLab (Blender
.blend with rigs + PBR textures), Steam Workshop GMod SWEPs (Source .mdl with full
FPS viewmodel + animation sets), or GitHub (Lohkas/IW4-Modeldump — all MW2

### 62. Firearm Gameplay — Hitscan + Voxel Destruction
Left-click fires: raycast from player eye along look direction (reuse existing
Tools::raycast_scene). Hit point triggers voxel carve (small radius, like Scalable
Dig) or debris body fracture (reuse impact fracture system). Ammo + fire rate
cooldown on VoxApp. Muzzle flash particle (reuse ParticleSystem). Weapon switch
via hotbar (slots 7-9). Recoil applied as camera pitch kick + viewmodel animation.
Reuses 100% of existing destruction/physics infrastructure — no new simulation.

### 63. Weapon Variety — Attachments & Materials
Data-driven weapon definitions (TOML, like material defs): damage, fire rate, ammo
capacity, recoil pattern, reload time, projectile type (hitscan vs projectile),
carve radius. Attachment system: scope (changes FOV/zoom), suppressor (no muzzle
flash, reduced recoil), extended mag. Voxel-material interaction: bullets penetrate
weak materials (leaves, glass) but stop on stone. Pairs with #16 chain-reaction
explosives for explosive ammo types.
